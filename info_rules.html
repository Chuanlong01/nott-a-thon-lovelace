Competition Rules

Registration


  1. Participants can join the competition in a team of three or less.
  2. Participants must register using the signup link by 1st February 2022.
  3. Participation fee is RM 30 per group. Whether there are only 1 or 3 members in a group, the fee is fixed.
  4. Participation fees are to payable to IET UNM’s Treasurer:


    5. Low Jia Yi
    205090445852
    Affin Bank Berhad

  5. Please upload your transaction slip when registering in the google form.
  6. An email will be sent when your payment is received. If you do not receive reply, kindly contact mobile number: +6012-696 8068.
  7. Participants who fail to register will not be eligible for prizes and rewards, and no certificate will be issued.
  8. Groups must be formed by the participants.
  9. Each group must submit a representative account username for submission, read further below.
  10. Please ensure the submitted username is correct. Wrong usernames that result in disqualifications will not be refunded.

Platform and Timeline


  1. Groups found to have violated the Foul Play Clauses in part C will be disqualified.
  2. Please contact efyjl10@nottingham.edu.my to report any foul play.
  3. The organizers reserve the right to disqualify any group for unacceptable misconduct or violation of the rules stipulated in this document.

Technical Support

  1. Before the competition, please email your queries (if any) to efyjl10@nottingham.edu.my .
  2. During the competition, you can contact the organizers on Discord, via this link:
  3. Do not share your codes or solutions in the competition Discord server.
  4. No compensation/refunds/time advantage/point advantage will be given for any technical issues.
  5. Please ensure you have a stable internet connection for the entire duration of the competition.

Questions


  1. There are seven challenges / questions throughout competition
    1. 3 Easy questions worth 10 points each.
    2. 3 Medium questions worth 20 points each.
    3. 2 Hard questions worth 30 points each.
  2. 5 test cases are set for easy and medium questions.
  3. 10 test cases are set for hard questions.
  4. Participants are free to choose the sequence of answering.
  5. Successfully answering each twest case will grant points.
  6. Please refer to part b[9] for the run time limit of each programing language.
